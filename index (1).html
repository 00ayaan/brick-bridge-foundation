Brick Bridge Foundation

Every brick
builds an engineer.

We turn donated LEGO sets into hands-on engineering kits, then teach kids to use them through in-person workshops and live virtual classes — building structural thinking, problem-solving, and real engineering fundamentals along the way.

How it works

From donation to discovery.

LEGO has been part of childhood for generations — kids are already excited to build. We channel that excitement into real engineering skills, in three steps.

01 / Donate

Bricks come in

Individuals, families, and stores donate LEGO sets — new, used, or surplus — instead of letting them sit unused.

02 / Build the kit

We design engineering kits

Every donation is turned into a structured kit built around a specific engineering concept, from load-bearing arches to simple machines.

03 / Teach

Kids learn by building

We lead kids through each kit step by step, turning play into an understanding of how and why things stay standing.

Our classes

Two ways to learn.

Every class is built around the same kits, so kids get the same hands-on engineering experience no matter how they join.

In-person

Workshops

Small-group sessions where kids build side by side with an instructor, working through a kit together and troubleshooting as they go.

Virtual

Live video classes

The same kit, mailed ahead of time, guided live over video — so kids anywhere can build along with the class in real time.
